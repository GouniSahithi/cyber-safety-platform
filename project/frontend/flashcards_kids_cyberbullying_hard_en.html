⚠️ Cyberbullying Awareness – Hard Level

Think before you act! Can you detect bullying? 🔍

Cards explored: 0 / 8 ⭐

👤 Someone shares your home address online. What is this called?
⚠️ This is doxxing. Report it immediately to parents and authorities.
📧 True or False: Blackmail online is just a joke.
❌ False. Blackmail is a crime and must be reported.
📱 A bully threatens to post your private photos. What should you do?
🚫 Do not respond. Save evidence and tell a trusted adult or police.
⚠️ What are signs of hard-level cyberbullying?
Doxxing, revenge posts, blackmail, and serious threats.
🔒 Why should you never share private photos online?
They can be misused by bullies to threaten or embarrass you.
👨‍🏫 Who should you report serious bullying to?
🙂 Parents, teachers, or cybercrime authorities.
🔍 True or False: It’s safe to fight back against bullies online.
❌ False. Fighting back can make it worse. Report instead.
📧 What is the safest way to handle blackmail?
Collect evidence, block the bully, and report to police.
